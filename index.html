xJüs - Flexible RHex

xJüs, the hexapodal robot with a passive backbone to improve behavior over harsh terrain.

Introduction

We are a team of five mechanical and electrical engineers, and for our senior design project at Princeton University we are designing and building a segmented robotic hexapod with a passively compliant spine. The goal of the project is to improve performance of the well-known RHex design over rough terrain and obstacles by incorporating a flexible backbone to allow for more natural motion, similar to the way that mammals and reptiles conform their bodies during locomotion.

Origin

Our team was intrigued by the RHex design from UPenn's KodLab and Boston Dynamics and wanted to build upon it. After discussing ideas with several professors, we met with one of the original designers of the RHex, and together reached the hypothesis that adding flexibility to the rigid design would improve performance and energy efficiency by enabling more natural gaits.

Current Progress

We are working concurrently on the hardware and software design. We have a completed CAD model and have full 3D simulations of our design's locomotion in a simulation environment using Blender, Python, and the Bullet physics engine. We have all of our COTS parts already, and have successfully demonstrated a two legged test cart in operation with two back casters. We keep our code, wiki, and issue tracker on a private GitHub repository, and our project is fully funded.
